# Email #19: Your Brain Can't Multitask

**Subject Line:** You're not multitasking — you're just switching fast and paying the cost

---

You're not multitasking. Your brain is rapidly switching between tasks — and every single switch costs you.

---

You're studying. Your phone buzzes. You glance at it for "just a second."

You go back to your notes. And for the next 10–15 minutes, part of your brain is still on whatever that notification contained.

This is called *attention residue* — and it's one of the most damaging forces in modern student life.

---

**Barbara Oakley explains the neuroscience in *A Mind for Numbers*:**

Your prefrontal cortex — the part responsible for deep thinking, analysis, and complex problem-solving — can only hold one cognitively demanding task at a time.

What we call "multitasking" is actually rapid task-switching. Each switch comes with a rebuilding cost — it takes an average of 23 minutes to fully regain deep focus after a significant distraction.

If you're checking your phone every 20 minutes, you are never — not once — actually reaching the depth of thinking where real learning happens.

You're not studying. You're repeatedly failing to start.

**A real test for you:**

Your next study session — put your phone in another room. Not on silent. Not face-down. Another room entirely.

Run your session. At the end, compare what you accomplished — the quality and depth of your thinking — to a session where your phone was present.

The difference will be stark. Most students are shocked by how much their phone was costing them in ways they never noticed.
