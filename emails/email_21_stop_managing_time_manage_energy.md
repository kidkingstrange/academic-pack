**Jim Loehr and Tony Schwartz, in *The Power of Full Engagement*, build the case:**

The problem is not time management. It's energy management.

Time is fixed. Energy is renewable — but only if you manage it deliberately. They identify four types of energy that high performers maintain: physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual (a sense of purpose and meaning).

When any one of these is depleted, performance across all areas suffers. Most students run on empty in all four — then blame themselves for not being disciplined enough.

Elite performers treat themselves like athletes. Hard training, strategic recovery, peak performance windows. Not constant output.

**Map your energy this week:**

For the next 5 days, track your energy level (1–10) at four points: morning, mid-morning, afternoon, and evening.

By Day 3, a pattern will emerge. You have a peak performance window — probably 2–3 hours where you are genuinely at your best cognitively.

Schedule your most demanding studying there. Use lower-energy periods for reading, admin, or lighter review. You'll accomplish more in fewer hours — and end the day less depleted.
