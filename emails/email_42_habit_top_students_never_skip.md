# Email #42: The 20-Minute Habit Top Students Never Skip

**Subject Line:** One habit. Twenty minutes. Every week. The results are remarkable.

---

I've worked with enough high-performing students to notice a clear pattern:

The ones who consistently outperform are not always the ones who study the most hours. They're the ones who review their week every Sunday for 20 minutes.

Without fail.

---

David Allen calls it the "cornerstone habit" of effective performance. Stephen Covey calls it "Sharpening the Saw." Both frameworks — *Getting Things Done* and *The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People* — converge on the same practice:

**Without a regular weekly review, you are always reacting. You never have the clarity to plan.**

The weekly review gives you the elevated view: what's been done, what's pending, what's coming, and what needs to change before the week ahead begins.

For students: a topic you're behind on is manageable in Week 2. It's a crisis in Week 10. The weekly review is how you catch it in Week 2.

---

**Your 20-minute Sunday review — starting this week:**

Answer these 5 questions. Write them down:

1. What did I accomplish this week academically?
2. What did I not complete — and why?
3. What are my top 3 academic priorities for next week?
4. Am I on track with my goals for this semester?
5. What one change would make next week more effective?

That's it. 20 minutes. Do it every Sunday.

This one practice, maintained for a full semester, will give you more clarity and control over your academic trajectory than any single study technique.
