# Email #33: Your Brain Is for Thinking, Not Storage

**Subject Line:** Every unfinished task in your head is quietly draining your focus

---

Every incomplete task, untracked deadline, and unresolved decision living in your head is silently consuming your mental energy right now.

You can't see the drain. But you feel it — as background anxiety, mental fog, and the constant low-level sensation that something important is about to slip through.

---

David Allen spent years studying why brilliant, capable people couldn't get on top of their workload — even when they had enough time and intelligence to handle it.

His diagnosis: it wasn't a time problem. It wasn't even a discipline problem.

It was a cognitive load problem.

**From *Getting Things Done*:**

Your mind is for having ideas, not storing them. The moment you write something down in a trusted external system — a planner, a task list, a structured calendar — your brain releases the effort of holding it. The cognitive load drops. The anxiety quiets. And suddenly you can think clearly about what's actually in front of you.

Most students are carrying their entire academic lives in their heads: every assignment, every deadline, every conversation to follow up on, every lecture to review, every appointment to keep.

That mental load is stealing the focus you need to actually learn.

**The brain dump (do this right now):**

Open a blank page. Set a timer for 10 minutes. Write down every incomplete task, upcoming deadline, worry, commitment, and open loop related to your studies. Don't organise it — just get it all out.

Then sort: What needs to happen today? This week? This semester?

The moment it's on paper, it stops living rent-free in your head. The mental energy it was consuming becomes available for actual thinking.
