# Email #16: Bruce Lee Only Had One Punch

**Subject Line:** One technique mastered beats ten techniques practised

---

Bruce Lee once said: *"I fear not the man who has practised 10,000 kicks once. I fear the man who has practised one kick 10,000 times."*

He wasn't talking about fighting. He was talking about how mastery actually works.

---

Most students are practising 10,000 kicks once.

They skim across the surface of every topic, never going deep enough into any single concept to truly own it. By exam time, they have a vague familiarity with everything and a complete command of nothing.

The student who scores highest isn't always the one who covered the most. It's the one who owned the most critical concepts completely.

**From *Striking Thoughts* — Bruce Lee's collected wisdom:**

Lee's philosophy of learning was surgical: identify what matters most, isolate it completely, and practise it until it becomes second nature.

Then — and *only* then — move to the next layer.

This principle, applied to academic study: know your syllabus well enough to identify the 20% of concepts that generate 80% of exam questions. Go devastatingly deep on those. Build them into rock-solid chunks before you spread your attention elsewhere.

Breadth impresses lecturers. Depth gets marks.

**Find your one punch this week:**

For each of your courses, identify the most foundational concept — the one that, if you truly mastered it, would make everything else easier.

Write it down. Spend the next three study sessions going relentlessly deep on just those concepts. Test yourself. Break it down. Explain it without notes. Apply it in problems.

Go deep before you go wide.
