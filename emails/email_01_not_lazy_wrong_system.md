**The science behind this:**

Barbara Oakley, in *A Mind for Numbers*, identifies the root of this problem precisely: your brain operates in two distinct modes — focused mode and diffuse mode. Most students only activate one. They grind in focused mode until exhaustion, never allowing the diffuse mode to consolidate what they've learned.

The result? Information goes in. But it doesn't stick. And no amount of extra hours changes that.

**What this means for you:**

Before your next study session, stop asking "Am I studying enough?" and start asking "Am I studying in a way that actually builds memory?"

Those are two completely different questions — and the second one is the only one that changes results.

Try this tonight: after 30 minutes of study, close your notes and write down everything you remember. Without looking. That discomfort you feel? That's your brain actually working.
