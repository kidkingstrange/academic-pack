# Email #38: Before You Can Change Your Grades, Change This

**Subject Line:** Two students. Same exam. The only difference is what they believe about it.

---

Two students walk into the same exam.

Student A believes exam results are largely down to luck — who marks the paper, which questions come up, whether you happen to remember the right things on the right day.

Student B believes exam results are a direct and predictable output of how you prepared in the weeks before.

Before a single answer is written, these two students have already produced different outcomes.

---

Not because of intelligence. Not because of effort. Because of the belief that shapes every preparation decision they make.

Student A doesn't start early — there's no point if it's mostly luck anyway. They study sporadically, hoping the right material comes up. They feel powerless going in.

Student B starts six weeks out. They study systematically, knowing that consistent preparation reliably produces results. They walk in with evidence of their own readiness.

Same exam. Wildly different experiences. Predictably different outcomes.

---

**Howard Gardner, in *Changing Minds*, studied what it actually takes to shift deeply held beliefs:**

The most powerful lever is not argument or information. It's resonance — a story or experience that connects emotionally with what you already sense to be true, but haven't been willing to act on.

And real-world events — a result, good or bad, that forces a re-examination of the story you've been telling yourself.

You have probably already had a result that should have prompted a re-examination.

**The question is whether you acted on it, or absorbed the result and continued the same approach.**

---

**What belief are you currently running about your academic performance?**

Write it down honestly. Not the one you'd say out loud to someone else. The one that runs quietly underneath your decisions.

Then ask: Is this belief based on accurate evidence — or on a story that formed when you were younger, less equipped, or using worse methods?

Beliefs that no longer serve you can be changed. But only if you're willing to examine them directly.
