---

**Chris McChesney and Sean Covey, in *The 4 Disciplines of Execution*, call this the "whirlwind problem":**

The whirlwind — the constant urgency of daily life — never stops. Lectures, assignments, social demands, personal challenges. It fills every available space.

If you wait for the whirlwind to slow down before making important changes, you will wait forever. Because it never slows.

The only way out is a deliberate decision made *while* the whirlwind is active.

Not when conditions are perfect. Because they never are.
