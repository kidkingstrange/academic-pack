What if the student spending 12 hours a day studying is at a disadvantage?

 

Scott Young's research into skill acquisition found a pattern across every high performer he studied: they didn't just practice more. They practised better.

Specifically — they used feedback loops. Immediate signals that told them, in real time, whether what they were doing was actually working.

The student grinding for 12 hours but never testing themselves gets no feedback. They have no idea whether the information is landing. They just accumulate hours and hope.

The student who studies 4 hours with constant self-testing, immediate error correction, and spaced review is getting three times the learning signal in one-third of the time.

Feedback is the most critical ingredient in skill development. Without fast, accurate feedback, effort doesn't compound. It just repeats.
